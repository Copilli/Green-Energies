Solar energy

Solar energy is energy obtained from the sun in the form of electromagnetic radiation (light, heat and ultraviolet rays). By installing solar panels or collectors, it can be used to obtain thermal energy (photothermal system) or to generate electricity (photovoltaic system).


Who discovered solar energy?

The history of solar energy dates back to Ancient Greece, when philosophers such as Aristotle and Plato wrote about solar energy. The first attempt to harness solar energy is believed to have been made by the Greek engineer and astronomer, Anaxagoras, in the 5th century BC. Anaxagoras proposed that a glass lens focused on a burning wooden surface could ignite it. This was the first time in history that solar energy was used to produce heat.


How is solar energy produced?

Solar energy can be captured through photoelectric cells (which make up the photovoltaic panels that we all know), heliostats or solar collectors, which subsequently transform it into thermal solar energy (through temperature) or photovoltaic solar energy (through light).
